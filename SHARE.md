# Project Description — Corpus Cluster Explorer

I'm building an interactive system that lets me cluster an entire web-text pre-training corpus (the full 10TB Nemotron-CC-v2) at any granularity from 5 to 1000+ topic clusters and see short LLM-generated descriptions within a few seconds of choosing a new `k`. My aim is to turn corpus-scale clustering from a multi-hour batch job into a tight feedback loop, so exploring how a real pre-training corpus partitions at different granularities becomes a normal part of designing data-mixing experiments. To get there I'm benchmarking and optimizing two pieces of the pipeline. The first is the embedding pass with Luxical-One, a recently released CPU-only model that runs about 100x faster than typical GPU transformer encoders, which I want to push hard enough to embed the full corpus on a single node. The second is k-means clustering, where I'm comparing sklearn and faiss on CPU and GPU and trying to amortize cost across `k` values, with the success metric being under 10 seconds end-to-end to re-cluster the full embedded corpus once it has been processed once.
